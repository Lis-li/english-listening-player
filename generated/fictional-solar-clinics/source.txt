Fictional Report: Solar Clinics Restore Health Services After Coastal Storm

Health teams in the fictional coastal region of Bellhaven have opened three mobile clinics powered by solar energy after a severe storm damaged the main hospital. The temporary units were installed beside schools and community halls, where residents can receive basic treatment without travelling across flooded roads.

Each clinic carries a compact laboratory, vaccine refrigerators, clean-water equipment, and a satellite connection for remote consultations. Nurses can test blood samples, speak to specialists in the regional capital, and update medical records even when the local electricity network is unavailable.

Officials said respiratory infections and stomach illnesses increased during the week after the storm. They also warned that interrupted medicine deliveries could place people with diabetes and heart conditions at greater risk. Local volunteers are now using small electric boats to carry prescriptions to isolated neighbourhoods.

The project was developed by Bellhaven University and the fictional relief group Horizon Health. Engineers designed the clinics to fit inside standard shipping containers, allowing them to be moved quickly by truck or boat.

Doctors say the mobile units cannot replace a full hospital, particularly for surgery or intensive care. However, they believe the clinics can prevent minor health problems from becoming emergencies while repair work continues. Regional leaders are considering keeping one unit permanently for future floods and other disasters.
